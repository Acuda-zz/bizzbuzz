About Baroy

Baroy is a 4th class municipality in the province of Lanao del Norte, Philippines. According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 24,683 people.

The municipality is known for its rich agricultural lands, beautiful landscapes, and warm, hospitable people. Baroy is composed of 23 barangays, each with its own unique character and charm.

Our vision is to make Baroy a model municipality in terms of sustainable development, good governance, and community empowerment.

Featured barangays

Poblacion

The town center and seat of the municipal government. Home to the municipal hall, public market, and main commercial area.

San Juan

A coastal barangay with fishing as the main livelihood. Offers beautiful beach views and fresh seafood.

Dalama

Known for its agricultural lands producing rice, corn, and various fruits. Features beautiful rural landscapes.

Village

A residential barangay near the town center. Known for its growing community and accessibility to schools and local facilities.

Pange

A rural barangay with a peaceful environment. Mostly agricultural, with wide open spaces and a strong sense of community.

Baroy Daku

A coastal barangay with fishing as a key livelihood. Features scenic views and a close-knit fishing community.
